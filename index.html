Roland Faure

My research lies at the intersection of biology and computer science, with a focus on developing algorithms for large-scale (meta)genomic data analysis. Since February 2025, I am a postdoctoral researcher in Rayan Chikhi's group at Institut Pasteur, Paris, working on efficient indexing and exploration of the Logan dataset. Previously, I completed my Ph.D. (2021–2024) on metagenome assembly and strain separation under the joint supervision of Dominique Lavenier (Inria Rennes) and Jean-François Flot (Université Libre de Bruxelles).

// SOFTWARE

Alice

[GitHub]
HiFi metagenomic assembler. Its originality is to introduce and use MSR sketching, which allows the assembler to assembler reduced sketches rather than original reads while distinguishing strains

SNooPy

[GitHub]
Long read metagenomic variant caller. Implements a novel statistical framework tailored for metagenomic, with no assumptions on coverage, strain evolutionnary history or number of haplotypes.

HairSplitter

[GitHub]
Maps long reads to an assembly, detects regions where haplotypes are collapsed, groups reads by haplotype, and reassembles each group separately. Yields an assembly where all haplotypes are separated.

Strainminer

[GitHub]
Same software as HairSplitter, with the core algorithm reformulated as an ILP problem solved with Gurobi (experimental).

GenomeTailor

[GitHub]
Correct an assembly graph by realigning long reads on it.

GraphUnzip

[GitHub]
Uses long reads and/or Hi-C to improve the contiguity of assembly graphs.

QuickDeconvolution

[GitHub]
Scalable solution for barcode deconvolution of 10x reads.
